# ECONOMICS — AI Spend Audit

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to Credex

A "converted lead" is a startup that books a consultation and purchases Credex infrastructure credits.

**Assumptions (conservative):**
- Average startup AI spend at point of conversion: $2,000/month
- Credex saves them 20–28% → $400–560/month in savings
- Credex charges a platform fee of ~15% of the savings delivered
- Monthly revenue per customer: $400 × 15% = $60/mo → $720/year
- Average customer lifetime: 18 months (startups grow, churn, or get acquired)
- **LTV per converted customer: ~$1,080**

At the high end (larger customers, $5k/mo spend):
- Savings: $1,000–1,400/mo
- Credex fee: $150–210/mo
- LTV: ~$2,700–3,780

**Blended LTV estimate: $1,500** (mixing small and mid-size customers)

## CAC at Each GTM Channel

| Channel | Est. conversion rate | CAC |
|---------|---------------------|-----|
| Reddit / organic | audit→lead: 5%, lead→customer: 15% | ~$0 cash, ~2hrs time |
| Existing Credex customers (warm) | audit→lead: 50%, lead→customer: 40% | ~$0 (account team time) |
| Newsletter mention (TLDR etc.) | audit→lead: 3%, lead→customer: 10% | ~$0 (free mentions) |
| X/LinkedIn organic | audit→lead: 4%, lead→customer: 12% | ~$0 cash |
| Paid search (future) | audit→lead: 8%, lead→customer: 20% | ~$150–300/customer |

At $0 paid budget, CAC is effectively the opportunity cost of the time spent on outreach — roughly $50–100 in founder/team time per customer acquired through organic channels.

**Payback period at organic CAC: < 1 month** (LTV $1,500 vs CAC ~$75)

## Conversion Funnel

```
Landing page visit
    ↓ 40% start audit
Audit form completed
    ↓ 85% reach results page
Results page viewed
    ↓ 30% submit email
Email captured (lead)
    ↓ 20% of high-savings leads book consultation
Consultation booked
    ↓ 50% purchase credits
Credit purchase (customer)
```

**Overall funnel: 1,000 visitors → 400 audits → 340 results → 102 emails → ~10 consultations (high-savings only) → ~5 customers**

At LTV $1,500: **1,000 visitors → $7,500 in pipeline**

This assumes ~30% of audits show >$500/mo savings (the Credex CTA threshold). That's a reasonable assumption for teams with 5+ seats across multiple tools.

## What Would Have to Be True for $1M ARR in 18 Months

**Target:** $1M ARR = ~$83k MRR = ~56 active customers at $1,500 LTV/18mo (or ~1,400 customers at $60/mo recurring)

The more realistic path is a mix:

**Path A — Volume (small customers):**
- 1,400 customers × $60/mo average = $1M ARR
- Requires: ~28,000 audits completed (5% audit→customer conversion)
- Requires: ~70,000 landing page visitors over 18 months (~4k/mo)
- Achievable with consistent content + SEO + word of mouth

**Path B — Enterprise (fewer, larger customers):**
- 100 customers × $833/mo average = $1M ARR
- Requires: ~2,000 audits from companies spending $5k+/mo on AI
- Requires: targeted outreach to Series B+ companies, not just organic
- Harder to achieve with $0 budget but higher LTV per customer

**Most likely path — hybrid:**
- 300 customers averaging $280/mo = $1M ARR
- Requires: ~6,000 audits, ~15,000 visitors/month by month 12
- Credex's existing customer base (warm leads) gets you to 50 customers fast
- Organic growth + 1–2 newsletter features gets you to 300 over 18 months

**What has to be true:**
1. The audit shows real, defensible savings — users trust the numbers
2. The shareable URL creates a viral loop (each user brings 1.5 more)
3. Credex's consultation team converts >40% of high-savings leads
4. Credex's credit product actually delivers the promised savings (retention)

The tool is a top-of-funnel asset. The economics only work if the underlying Credex product delivers — the audit creates the expectation, Credex has to meet it.
